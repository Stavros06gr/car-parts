Cooling System

The cooling system regulates the engine temperature by removing excess heat, preventing overheating, and maintaining optimal performance.

Mechanical Process

  • Radiator: Dissipates heat from the coolant into the air.
  • Water Pump: Circulates coolant through the engine and radiator.
  • Thermostat: Regulates coolant flow based on engine temperature.
  • Coolant: Fluid that absorbs and transfers heat away from the engine.

Core Components

  • Radiator: Heat exchanger that cools the engine coolant.
  • Water Pump: Mechanical pump that moves coolant through the system.
  • Thermostat: Valve that opens and closes to control coolant flow.
  • Cooling Fan: Helps draw air through the radiator to improve cooling.
